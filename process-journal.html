Process Journal

Introduction

At first we envisioned a lot of unmanned tools, such as unmanned school buses, unmanned express delivery, unmanned motorhomes, and unmanned mobility scooter. In the end, we adopted the unmanned mobility scooter, because the unmanned scooter is relatively new and has the needs of users, has been unanimously recognized by the team members.

1. Background Research




Next, we conducted a survey on the specific applicable crowds and locations of unmanned mobility scooter. Among them, I focus on the location used, and my team members focus on the applicable users.

Figure 1.1 - Background Research

Location

In the location, we surveyed 100 users through questionnaires and found the most popular locations, namely hospitals, streets, airports, and attractions. The percentages were 35%, 37%, 51%, and 62% respectively.

Scenic-62%

The sights are really attractive when I first thought about it. We all want to let the tour relax our mood. If you have a scooter, it will definitely make our journey more enjoyable. Even if many users choose the scenic spot, there are still many problems in implementing the unmanned mobility scooter in the scenic spot. There are many real problems that we have not considered. There are a series of problems when the openness of the attractions is limited. For example, there may be road congestion, damage to ancient buildings, and encounter stairs. The solution I thought of at the time was that it was only for a small number of elderly and disabled people. After our group discussion of the second assessment , we found that although the attraction can supply a small number of people , the risk factor is still high , and the function of software development is also less .Since the achievability of the attraction project is very small, we gave up on this idea.

Airport-51%

In the initial stage, I think the airport is a very suitable place for unmanned mobility scooters. Because the airport terrain is empty and flat, users always take some luggage. At this time, I think we need a scooter to help us alleviate our burden. Help us take some luggage. Even if we need to walk through the long terminal, don't be too tired, because we have a scooter that can replace our walk. We even made some sketches about the airport, such as taking a ticket to pick up the unmanned mobility scooters, etc., just when we thought about how to use the scooter to check the baggage and pass the security check, we have some problems, such as how to use the scooter to checked baggage when you use a scooter, do you want to use it? If you use a scooter to queue up, it will cause road congestion. If you only use the scooter to transport the baggage, it will cause loss. Another type of safety check will cause the car to change. We originally wanted to use the scooter to provide convenience to people, but in these respects, the scooter seems to be no longer convenient.

Street-37%

In the initial planning, I saw the existing design, shared the bicycle, and began to imagine that we can let the unmanned scooter also share, which may provide convenience for people, but when doing the interview, many users said that it is convenient. But it may cause road congestion and is less secure. In terms of dealing with this, we think it might be possible to make the user less, for example, only for the elderly or disabled.

Hospital-35%

We think the hospital may be more appropriate because many of the users here are not free to move. If you only want to take a wheelchair, wheelchairs are not convenient in many situations. You may need the power of others to move. This reflects this. The importance of driverless mobility scooter is that the hospital is very busy at some point, so it is very unsuitable for relatively large scooters. In this regard, it remains to be verified.

Location Summary-

From the above point of view, although the scenics and airports are more popular, but the realities and the expansion of the follow-up software are not ideal and in some respects conflict with the ideas of the group members, the two locations are not considered for the time being. In the street area, I proposed a sharing plan (taking the existing design sharing bicycle as an example, I want users to also use shared unmanned mobility scooters.), but the negative impact will be a lot, so I gave up the idea, and finally we decided to we decided to focus on the streets and hospitals.


Applicable users

Most participants believe that elderly and disabled people may need unmanned mobility scooters, and there are many reports that older people are the largest user group of scooters. According to the user's survey, the affinity map shows that the unmanned mobility scooter can achieve health management, location tracking, security and convenience etc.


Summary

We will focus on using users on the elderly. We interviewed 8 users. Many users think that it may be too dangerous on the street. If you can't control the speed or other situations, the result will be very unsatisfactory, but the other location hospital is not exactly in line with our users, so we have created a new location - nursing home

2. Software


Original design intention

After the background investigation, we thought of its expansion, the scooter software - the software installed in the scooter tablet. A software that better protects the applicable population (elderly), including control speed, maps, health management, clinics, etc. When using a mobility scooter, we can't automate the first step with just the button. We hope that the scooter is more convenient and more practical, because our main audience is the elderly people, so we designed a software for the elderly to give it some special features, such as helping users measure heartbeat blood pressure and statistical walking steps, and can send it to doctors and family members at any time. Sometimes older people may not be able to see the things clearly, so a voice service is established. In many cases, elderly are not good at using mobile phones, so the family often does not know the location of the elderly. We can use the scooter software to turn on the positioning settings so that the family can know the location of the old man at any time.


Challenges

According to the feedback from users, we can find that there are many functions that we can expand. For example, we can improve some functions on our software, such as calendar function and drug reminder. The function of medicine used every day can also be Existing functional health management and clinic connections. Enhance some of the features in the map like positioning systems and sending your location to your family. There are many users who own a unmanned car at home. Maybe we can connect the unmanned mobility scooter to the car at home.

We may not temporarily give up the position of the street, because there are some dangers in the street, but the demand for potential users is still very high. We can develop some features in the software to reduce the user's concerns, such as the map positioning system, and the security measures that can be adjusted at any time or in the event of excessive heart rate or other conditions.

3. Prototype evolution and testing

We change our model based on existing online reports and user interviews.


Interface sketches & Lo-fidelity mockups & Wireframes
Figure 1 - Sketch

We use a tablet size instead of mobile software because the phones are mostly too small and complex for older people. In Campbell’s(2016) research said generally application’s designs for elderly avoid font sizes smaller than 16 pixels. Printable content is super useful for people with vision and perception problems. So in our software all use more than 16px text In some cases, users may not know the default rules already available, such as scroll bars, so I can use the arrow directly to click on the switch page.

We also got some feedback through user testing.

First group of users

The first thing they clicked was the voice function. They thought that the voice function was really convenient, for example, they could go directly to the interface they wanted. They think the health management interface is very useful, but they are considering whether to disclose privacy when sending management information to their private doctor or family. They don't know how to return to the previous interface when they quit. Then they click on the map page, and one of them said that the location of themselves on the map indicated that was not very obvious. They think that the voice function is designed very well because the old people often can't the screen clearly. Just hope that the voice function can be prompted first. Otherwise, the elderly may not know that they are already using it.

Second group of users

They have some troubles about how to return to the previous interface, and there is a button on our main page that shows the entire screen, but they don't know what the icon means. In the health management interface, in the initial low-fidelity model, the blood pressure heartbeat and step number icons are not obvious, they haven’t in a column, which can be confusing to the user. When they enter the map page, they prefer to adjust the speed and display time in this interface. In the menu bar next to it, there is only a stop button but no emergency button. They think that at some point only the stop button is useless. They think there can be an emergency button for help.




Third group of users

First they clicked to go to the clinic, but the problem was that they thought the speed of the scooter was too fast. Walking on the street might be dangerous. In addition, they said that they can't go home directly in this map, they want to go home… They told us that the weather system in the upper left corner was doing very well.

Users and feedback

Mainly old people, some students with design background and designers. We could easily find in Wilkinson building while they gave many suggestions about the visual style and typesetting. Invision was used in the test, the user seat in a chair pretends an old person riding AMS.


Buttons

On most pages we use the 2d style, but we use the 3d style on some important buttons, there are many users suggest us that we should use the faded color to reduce the awkwardness. We designed the “help” button with a gradient color to make it obvious for user, so we changed it with a darker color, but it is still a gradient color. The big "back" key affects the overall aesthetics so we use smaller one.


The state of health

After the test, only "very healthy" is displayed on the left side of the screen, and the left layout is very monotonous. Which makes the entire page looking inharmonious. We both agreed this opinion, so we change the layout of the page and added an emoji under “very healthy”.


Map

If the map can be enlarged, it can make the user see more clearly, and add the positioning function to let the user know the current location.


Color

Based on some reports, we get the color that most older people like and use, and then we put these colors in our high-fidelity model. Specific colors and instructions are detailed in the report and on the companion website.)


Personal reflection

In my opinion, our high-fidelity model still has a big gap with the actual software already in existence.

Design style

Using too many different colors. Our original intention is to let the elderly people get visual reminders through the color, but too many colors have caused visual clutter, we should probably think about it how to solve this problem.

Interactive experience

Due to limited time, we didn't open all the features in the software, and I think we might be able to connect our software to some existing websites like google and facebook.
